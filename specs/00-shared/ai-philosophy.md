**Project:** Requira
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Approved
**Owner:** AI Engineering
**Document Type:** Shared AI Philosophy

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the principles that govern how Artificial Intelligence is used within Requira.

The goal is to ensure AI remains reliable, explainable, and useful while supporting software teams during requirement analysis.

AI is a capability of Requira, not its identity.

Every AI workflow implemented in the product should align with the philosophy described in this document.

---

# 2. AI's Role in Requira

Artificial Intelligence exists to assist software teams in reviewing requirement artifacts.

It should help users:

* Identify potential gaps
* Highlight risks
* Detect ambiguity
* Suggest clarification questions
* Recommend improvements
* Support development readiness assessments

AI should never attempt to replace Business Analysts, Product Managers, or Project Managers.

---

# 3. Human-in-the-Loop

Requira is designed as a decision-support platform.

The AI should:

* Analyze
* Explain
* Recommend

The human should:

* Review
* Validate
* Decide

Final ownership of every project decision always belongs to the project team.

---

# 4. Evidence-First Philosophy

Every meaningful AI output should be supported by evidence.

Evidence may come from:

* Uploaded requirement artifacts
* Retrieved knowledge from the RAG system
* Recognized requirement engineering practices

Recommendations without supporting evidence should not be shown to users.

---

# 5. Explainability

Every recommendation generated by Requira should answer four questions:

* What was found?
* Why does it matter?
* What evidence supports it?
* What should the user consider next?

Users should never receive unexplained AI outputs.

---

# 6. Recommendations, Not Decisions

Requira should never present conclusions as absolute facts.

Instead of:

> "The project is ready."

The platform should communicate:

> "Based on the available requirements, the project appears ready for development review."

This distinction preserves user trust and reinforces human ownership.

---

# 7. Confidence

Every important finding should include a confidence level.

Confidence communicates how strongly the available evidence supports the assessment.

Confidence does not represent certainty.

Users should be encouraged to review low-confidence findings carefully.

---

# 8. Retrieval Before Generation

AI should always retrieve relevant context before generating recommendations.

The workflow should follow this principle:

Requirement Artifact

↓

Knowledge Retrieval

↓

Context Building

↓

AI Analysis

↓

Recommendation

Retrieval reduces hallucinations and produces more grounded outputs.

---

# 9. Structured AI Workflows

Requira avoids using one large prompt to perform every task.

Instead, AI responsibilities are divided into focused stages such as:

* Requirement Extraction
* Classification
* Gap Detection
* Risk Analysis
* Clarification Question Generation
* Test Scenario Generation
* Development Readiness Assessment

This improves consistency and makes the workflow easier to maintain.

---

# 10. Responsible AI

AI should:

* Be transparent
* Be explainable
* Avoid unsupported assumptions
* Respect user input
* Produce professional outputs
* Acknowledge uncertainty

The system should avoid overstating its confidence or presenting speculative information as fact.

---

# 11. Continuous Improvement

Requira's AI should improve over time through:

* Better knowledge frameworks
* Improved prompts
* Enhanced workflows
* Better evaluation methods
* User feedback

The goal is to improve recommendation quality rather than simply increase AI complexity.

---

# 12. AI Principles Summary

Every AI feature added to Requira should satisfy the following principles:

* AI assists rather than replaces people.
* Retrieve knowledge before generating recommendations.
* Every recommendation should be explainable.
* Every important finding should include evidence.
* Confidence should always be communicated.
* Recommendations should support human judgment.
* Simplicity is preferred over unnecessary AI complexity.

These principles define how AI is expected to behave throughout the Requira platform and should guide every future AI enhancement.
